Scoring Pattern and Logic 


🚦 First, understand the structure (super simple)
Big 5 test basics:
* 50 questions
* Each answer = 1 to 5
* 5 traits
* 10 questions per trait
* Each trait score range = 0 to 40
So:
👉 10 items × max 5 = 50
👉 But formulas include subtraction + constants
👉 Final standardized range becomes 0–40
________________


🧠 The Key Concept (most important)
Each trait uses:
Formula pattern:
Trait Score = Base Constant + (Add some questions) − (Subtract some questions)


That’s it.
No fancy stats.
Just addition and subtraction.
________________


🔁 Why subtraction exists (reverse scoring)
Some questions are negatively worded.
Example:
* “Don’t talk a lot” (low extraversion)
If candidate answers 5 → actually means LOW extraversion.
So we subtract that score to flip the meaning.
👉 In tech terms: reverse scoring
________________

🧑‍💻 Now let’s build the algorithm
Here’s clean pseudocode you can directly implement:
________________


🔹 Generic Scoring Function
def compute_trait(responses, base, add_list, sub_list):
    score = base
    
    for q in add_list:
        score += responses[q]


    for q in sub_list:
        score -= responses[q]


    return score


________________


🔹 Big 5 Implementation

👉 Yes. Every number inside those formulas is simply the candidate’s actual answer (1–5). Nothing else.

🔍 What those numbers really mean
When you see something like:
E = 20 + (1) − (6) + (11) − (16) ...


Those bracket numbers are:
👉 Question numbers
NOT scores
NOT weights
NOT constants
They simply say:
“Take the score the person marked for that question.”
________________


🧠 Translate into plain English
Example:
+ (1)


means:
👉 Add the person’s answer to Question 1
If they selected:
* Disagree → 1

* Slightly agree → 4

* Agree → 5

You add exactly that number.
Nothing modified.
________________

⚠️ Then why subtraction?
Because some statements are negatively phrased.
Let’s look at:
Q1:
“I am the life of the party”
High score → high extraversion
So → ADD
Q6:
“I don’t talk a lot”
High score → LOW extraversion
So → SUBTRACT
We subtract to reverse the psychological direction.
________________


🧠 Think like a system designer
Internally, your engine is simply doing:
trait_score = base
for positive questions:
    trait_score += response


for negative questions:
    trait_score -= response


That’s all.
So:
👉 responses are the real raw scores (1–5)
 👉 formulas just decide whether to add or subtract them
________________
